After graduating from high school in 1960, ethologist Jane Goodall was sent to Africa to observe the chimpanzees by a British paleontologist Dr. Louis Leakey. She found that the chimpanzees could break the grass and made a grass stick. They stuck these sticks into the ant nests and waited for the ants to climb on the grass sticks. The chimpanzees would then pulled out these sticks and licked the sticks eating the ants as food. Goodall asked this question: could these chimpanzees be humans? Up to now, the 100 year old definition that humans were “animals who could create and use tools”  is completed demolished by Goodall’s observation.
