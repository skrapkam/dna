一.DNA、染色体和线粒体

I. DNA, chromosome and mitochondria

 

1.     存储生命遗传信息唯一的载体

1. The only carrier storing genetic information of life

 

现代生物分子学说已经确定，动物和植物“细胞中的DNA编码，是存储生命遗传信息唯一的载体”。同时确定了这些DNA编码，是存在于细胞中的染色体和线粒体的内部。

 

Modern theory of molecular biology has already established that “the DNA codes in cells of both animals and plants are the only carrier storing genetic information of life”. Meanwhile, it is accepted that these DNA codes exist in chromosomes and mitochondria of the cells.

 

有关DNA的生物化学结构，在本书第2章有比较详细的介绍。在讨论物种起源的课题时，您完全可以简单地认为：生物的DNA编码就是一串串由4个字符组成的编码。人类46条染色体就是46条基本保持恒定的46串DNA编码，线粒体就是一个基本保持恒定的环状DNA编码，人类的全部遗传信息就包含在它们之中。当然，和DNA编码在一起的，还有一些链接和支持DNA的其它物质。4个类型的DNA编码（A、T、C、G）就像被串起的上亿的串珠，并因为它们被严格地编制成的序列，准确的表达着它们承载的遗传信息。

 

Section 2 of this Book describes in detail about biological and chemical structure of DNAs. Speaking of the origin of species, just remember that biological DNA codes are strings of codes composed of 4 characters. The 46 human chromosomes are basically 46 constant strings of DNA codes, and mitochondria are basically constant ring-shaped DNA codes which contain all genetic information. Certainly, together with DNA codes are also some links and other material supporting DNAs. The 4 types of DNA codes (A, T, C and G), liken to hundreds of millions of beads stringed together, can accurately express the genetic information they carry by their strictly programmed sequences.

 

DNA的4个编码是A、T、C、G，代表4个碱基，它们是构成生命的4个“砖块”，每一个“砖块”并没有多大意义，特定排列的组合――编码序列，才编织出了各个物种的生命。

 

The 4 codes of DNA( i.e. A, T, C and G) represent 4 nucleobases as 4 “bricks” composing life. Each “brick” is not very Significance, but their unique combinations, i.e. coding sequence, which weaves together the lives of all living species.

 

从各个物种的细胞中，取得这些序列是一个非常复杂困难的工作。所幸，由于自动检测的设备不断地改进，测定DNA编码序列成为越来越简单的事情。当编码一但输入基因银行以后，DNA信息已经抽象成一串串的编码。面对这些编码的比较和分析所进行的生物遗传信息研究，已经从面向生物机理的研究转变成为数学和计算机学科分析的范畴了。由此，出现了一个新的学科：现代生物信息学。从目前得到的资料来看，有关现代生物信息学的课程大多是在计算机相关的大学专业中设置。

 

It is a complicated and involved a lot of hard work to obtain these sequences from cells of each species. Fortunately, thanks to constant improvement of automatic test equipment, it has become easier and easier to determine DNA coding sequences. When the codes are entered into GenBank, the DNA information is translated into strings of codes. Biological genetic information research in comparing and analyzing these codes has turned from research of biological mechanism to mathematics and computer-based analyses. As a result, a new discipline, Modern Biological Information Science, has emerged. Current information shows that the most college courses about modern biological information science are related to computer.

 

2.     基因和基因组

2. Genes and genomes

 

基因：每个DNA分子(染色体)含有很多个基因，一个基因只是一小段DNA的序列。其序列的长度从上千到2万多个字符。目前发现的人类基因大约有3万多个，每一个基因都包含有它特定的遗传信息。

 

Genes: Each DNA molecule (chromosome) contains many genes, which each is a small stretch of DNA sequences. Length of each sequence is from 1,000 to over 20,000 characters. So far, over 30,000 human genes have been identified, each containing unique genetic information.

 

人类基因组：人类染色体基因组是指细胞核中全套染色体（24条：22条常染色体、X染色体、Y染色体）全部的DNA分子序列。这24个DNA分子序列总共有大约30.96亿个（3,096MB）DNA编码。其中，包含的3万多个基因的序列总长，只占基因组DNA序列的3%左右。其它区域中的DNA编码包含的生理意义未明，常被冠上一个不恰当的名字―DNA“沙漠区”（或称“垃圾区”）。基因组既包含基因区，又包含“沙漠区”，这就是基因和基因组的区别。在本书中有时用“全部DNA”来代替“基因组”这个词汇。“基因组”千万不要误认为是基因的组合，基因只是基因组很小的组成部分。

 

Human genome: Human chromosome genome refers to all DNA molecule sequences of all chromosomes (24 pieces: 22 autosomes, X chromosome and Y chromosome) in a cell nucleus. The 24 DNA molecule sequences have about 3.096 billion (3,096MB) DNA codes. The length of over 30,000 gene sequences accounts for about 3% of the genome’s DNA sequences. DNA’s “desert” (or “junk”) region often refer to areas where the DNA codes contain undefined physiological significance. The genome contains both gene area and “desert area” as different from genes. In this book, “genome” is replaced by “all DNAs” in this book. Never mistaken “genome”  as the combination of genes, which constitute only a small part.

 

在人类的线粒体中，还包含有16569个编码的DNA序列，在2章中有很详细的说明，不再重复。

 

The human mitochondria also contain DNA sequences of 16,569 codes. We will not repeat it here as detailed in Part 2.

 

3.     人类的染色体

3. Human chromosomes

 

46条(23对)染色体是细胞核中最重要的组成部分，染色体是由DNA和蛋白质组成的丝状结构，因为可以被一些碱性染料着色而得名。人体细胞中46条染色体DNA分子总长度达2米，平均每条染色体DNA分子长度约5厘米，细胞的直径大约是10微米。

 

The 46 pieces (or 23 pairs) of chromosomes, named after a basic dye stuff, constitute the most important part of cell nucleus as filamentary structures of DNAs and protein. The DNA molecules of the 46 chromosomes are 2 meters long in total. Each chromosome averages about 5 centimeters. The diameter cell is about 10 μm.

 

在生物细胞分裂过程中，染色体的形态会发生周期性的变化。细胞分裂过程可以划分为间期、分裂期。间期染色体处于丝状松散状态，它开始只包含一条DNA分子（有此染色体全部DNA编码），通过复制后，形成两条相同的DNA分子，称为姐妹染色体单体（sister chromatid）。姐妹染色体单体彼此之间以着丝粒（centromere）相连。

 

During cell division, the shapes of chromosomes may have periodical change. The cell division process includes interphase and division phase. During interphase, the chromosomes are loose filaments. In the beginning, it covers only one DNA molecule (with all DNA codes of the chromosome). Through copying, it becomes two identical DNA molecules, known as the sister chromatid, Centromere connects every two sister chromatids.

 

在分裂期的前期，染色体逐步收缩变短变粗，在显微镜下可以看到它们呈现为X状态（见图11-1）。随后，姐妹染色体分离成两个棒状的染色体（见图11-2），进入细胞分裂的过程。细胞分裂期时段所占时间很短，因此，细胞在大多数的时期中，内部的染色体一点也不像 X 形状，而是分散开的单个丝状或短粗棒状形态的染色体。

 

At early division phase, the chromosomes gradually shrink and become shorter and thicker, X-shaped under the microscope (see Figure 11-1). Later, the sister chromatids divide into two club-shaped chromosomes (see 11-2) and start cell division. Cell division takes a short time, so the internal chromosomes appear not X-shaped but as dispersed filaments or short/thick clubs during the most period of the cell.

每个染色体的着丝粒将染色体划分为短臂（p）和长臂（q）两部分。在短臂和长臂的末端分别有一特别部位称为端粒，在第6章中有详细的介绍。

 

Centromere of each chromosome divides it into short arm (p) and long arm (q). At the ends of both short arm and the long arm is a special position respectively known as telomere (see Part 6 for details).

 

依据着丝粒的位置，人类染色体可以分为三种：（1）近中着丝粒染色体，着丝粒位于或靠近染色体中央，将染色体分为长短相近的两个臂；(2)亚中着丝粒染色体，着丝粒偏于一端，将染色体分为长短明显不同的两个臂；(3)近端着丝粒染色体，着丝粒非常靠近染色体的一个顶端。人类近端着丝粒染色体的短臂末端有一球状结构，称为随体。（See Part 7）

 

There are three types of telomere in human chromosome depending on the locations of centromere: (1) sub- metacentric chromosome, where the centromere is located or close to the center of chromosome, which divides the chromosome into two different arms of similar length; (2) submetacentric chromosome, where the centromere is located or close to the center of chromosome, which divides the chromosome into two different arms of distinctly different length; (3) telocentric chromosome very close to a tip of chromosome. At the end of short arm of the human telocentric chromosome is a ball-shaped structure, known as satellite (see Part 7).

Before DNA coding sequences of genome are firmly established, scientists tried to understand and mark chromosomes by banding technique and through the use of a microscope. The chromosome specimens are processed by certain program and stained with a certain dye stuff and then horizontal stripes will appear with different brightness and darkness along the long axle, known as chromosomal band. The means of band display is called banding technique. So far, chromosome banding technique is still the key method of judging chromosome abnormality in medical diagnosis. 上世纪70年代以来，显带技术得到了很大发展，且在众多的显带技术中（Q带、G带、C带、R带、T带），G带是目前被广泛应用的一种带型。G显带使用的染料名称是Giemsa，因此称之为G显带。人类染色体的G显带，可以在一条染色体上，从显微镜观察到850条左右深、浅的色带。

 

Since 1970s, the banding technique has achieved significant development. Among numerous banding techniques (including band Q, G, C, R and T), band G is a widely applied band. The dyestuff adopted by band G is Giemsa, after which band G is named. About B and G of human chromosomes, about 850 lighter or darker color bands can be observed under microscope on a single chromosome.

1.     各物种DNA总量恒定性和染色体的恒定性

4.  Constancy of total DNAs and chromosomes of all species

 

早在上世纪50年代，由美国生物学家H .Swift等人提出的“C值学说”(the concept of the “C-value”)指出：高等生物细胞核中染色体数目、DNA的含量（DNA编码的总数）保持一定的数值。这里的‘C’ 表达着恒定或特征的意义（constant or characteristic ）。以后对DNA基因组大量数据的研究，表明这个结论是正确的。美国著名分子生物学家Dan E. Krane 等的著作“生物信息学的基本概念” [注释 1]中国大学的“现代分子生物学”[注释 2]的教本等书中，都载入了这些内容。

 

Early in the 1950s, American.biologist H. Swift and others suggested the concept of the “C-value”: The chromosome number and DNA content in the nucleus of higher living creatures maintain certain value ‘C’, which means “constant” or “characteristic”. Later, a large quantity of data about DNA genome indicates that the conclusion is correct. “Fundamental Concepts of Bioinformatics”, the work by Dan E. Krane, the well-known U.S. Molecular Biologist, and Michael L. Raymer (Reference 1), as well as “Modern Molecular Biology”, (Reference 2),the textbook of Renmin University of China, also introduce this concept.

 

 

图11-6 “C值学说”，染色体恒定图

Figure 11-6  “C-value Theory” and Constant Chromosomes

 

 

动物的DNA编码绝大多数存在于染色体中，染色体数目的恒定和DNA总量恒定性，也就意味着染色体的恒定。这种恒定性包括：

 

Most DNA codes of animals exist in chromosomes. The constancy of chromosome number and total DNAs also indicate constancy of chromosomes. This constancy covers:

 

（1）染色体数目是恒定的：例如，人是23对（46条）染色体，如果出现非23对染色体的婴儿，例如是47条染色体，那么这就是患染色体疾病的孩子。

 

(1) Chromosome number: e.g. human beings have 23 pairs (or 46 pieces) of chromosomes. If some child does not have 23 pairs of chromosome, e.g. 47 pieces, he or she would be a child with a chromosomal disease.

 

（2）染色体包含的DNA编码的总数量是恒定的；

(2) The total DNA codes contained in a chromosome are constant in number;

 

（3）各染色体长度是恒定的：全世界人的22对常染色体、Y染色体、X染色体和线粒体的长度都是相等的，说明从一对父母，经过20几万年、上万代的繁衍，染色体的长度始终是恒定的。只是，在每一个人自身的生命历程内，染色体的端粒长度会逐渐在变短。奇妙的是，即使是年老男人的精子，其中染色体的端粒长度，和年轻男人的端粒长度却完全一样，因此新生婴儿的端粒仍然是最长的状态。这也意味着各染色体中的DNA编码总量，在各代中总保持着恒定。

 

(3) The length of all chromosomes is constant: The 22 pairs of autosomes, Y chromosomes, X chromosomes and mitochondria of all the peoples in the world have equal lengths, which indicate that a couple has kept a constant chromosome length for over 200,000 years and ten thousands of generations of breeding. However, within the life span of an individual, the telomere length of chromosome may gradually get shorter. It is amazing that for even the sperms of an old man, the telomere length of chromosome is entirely the same as that of a young man in length. As a result, the telomeres of a newborn baby still to keep the longest length. It also indicates that the total DNA codes of all chromosomes are kept constant in all generations.

 

染色体长度的恒定性在于DNA严格的拷贝，即使有极少DNA编码的突变，这种突变大多数是一个字符转变为另一个字符。即使有少数插入和缺失的突变，在数量加减相抵消后，其微乎其微的变化并不影响总体的恒定性。

 

The constancy of chromosome length lies in strict DNA replication process despite mutation of very few DNA codes, where one character may be changed to another. Even with a little mutation due to insertion or excision, the change is very meager and offset by the process of addition and loss. The overall constancy will not be impacted by these very minute changes.

 

（4）染色体结构的恒定性：每条染色体上只有一个着丝粒（着丝点），且位置恒定，它的位置常用作描述染色体的一个标识。

 

(4) Constancy of chromosome structure: Each chromosome has only one centromere with a constant location which is often used as a mark to describe chromosomes.

 

（5）基因在各染色体的分布上也是恒定的。

(5) The distribution of genes on all chromosomes is constant.

 

本书内容中的P公式、染色体数目是进化的鸿沟以及DNA编码遗传差异减半定律，都能和“C值学说”彼此相互印证与支持。

 

The “Formula P”, “Number of Chromosomes is the Gap of Evolution” and The law “the difference of DNA coding being one half” can all prove and lend support to the “C-value Theory” or vice versa.

 

染色体的恒定性并不表示在生物的繁衍中DNA编码完全保持不变，如果那样，人类就完全一模一样了。只是这种变化是十分微小的，它不会影响到染色体数目和长度的改变，也不会影响到着丝粒位置的改变。

 

The constancy of chromosomes does not indicate that DNA codes remain completely unchanged in the breeding of living creatures. If so, human beings would have been completely the same. However, the very meager change has no effect on the number and the length of chromosomes，as well on the centromere position.

 

那些DNA编码的微小改变就是DNA编码的突变，这种突变绝大多数是从一个编码改变为另一个编码，例如由C突变为G。目前已经测定了一千多人样本的基因组，平均DNA编码的差异大约是千分之一。也就是在你身上60亿个DNA编码上，平均在1000个编码序列段上，有一个字符和其他的人不相同。而和人类最早的祖先―“科学亚当”的差异，仅仅是千分之0.5，这是本书首先提出的“DNA编码差异减半定律”得到的结果(see Part 2)。

 

The meager change in the DNA codes is a mutation, where one DNA code may change to another (e.g. C to G) in most cases. So far, the genome of over one thousand samples has been determined with difference in about one in one thousand DNA code. This indicates that among the 6 billion DNA codes in your body, one character is different from that of others in 1000 code sequences. The difference with “Scientific Adam”, the earliest ancestor of human beings, is only 0.5 part per thousand, which is the result of The law “the difference of DNA coding being one half”(see Part 2).

 

5．C值悖理(C value paradox)，悖理吗？

5. C value paradox, really?

 

从以往进化论的生物理论上来看，基因组的大小应该同生物在进化上所处的地位及复杂性有对应的关系。然而，当更多物种的C值被检测出后，发现并非完全依照这个规律。甚至有的生物如肺鱼，身体结构简单，在进化论的理念中，也处在生物发展很低的地位，其C值却远高于人类。于是，生物研究人员称这种现象为C值悖理(C―value paradox)。

 

According to the past biological concepts of evolution, the size of genome should be related to the position and complicacy of living beings in evolution. However, when the C values of more and more living beings are worked out, the above concept is found not completely true. Even some living creatures such as lungfish, with a simple body structure, have a very low position in biological development according to the Theory of Evolution, but they have a far higher C value than human beings. As a result of this kind of observation, biological researchers believe that the phenomenon is a C value paradox.

 

应该看到，C值悖理并不是对C值恒定理论的否定，相反是肯定了同一物种各个体的C值是恒定的。只是认为不同物种C值的大小排列“与进化论的常理不符”。从本书的观点―物种之间不存在进化的联系和物种各从其类，C值的大小本身就与所谓物种进化程度无关，就不是悖理了。至于简单身体结构的生物为什么有更大的C值，有学者提出：“这暗示着真核生物基因组中必然存在大量的不编码的DNA序列，这些序列的功能很可能就是对基因的表达进行调控，但是还没有形成一套完整的理论。”[注释3]当发现科学事实和某种理论相违背时，必须去探讨这个理论本身是否正确，对待进化论就应该如此。

 

According to the above, the C value paradox affirms that all individuals of the same species have constant C value rather than refute the C value constancy theory. It believes that size ranking of C values of different living creatures does not confer to “the rules of Theory of Evolution”. According to opinion expressed in this Book, i.e. “no evolution connection between species” and “According to its kind”, the size of C value is not related to the degree of species’ evolution. Thus, this  is nota paradox. Why do living creatures of simple body structures have higher C values? Some scholar suggested, “It hints that genomes of eukaryotes must contain a large quantity of nonprogrammed DNA sequences, which are very likely to control expression of genes, but no complete theory has been formed yet.” (Reference 3) When some scientific facts are found in conflict with some theory, we shall analyze whether the theory is correct, which should lead to an attitude against the Theory of Evolution.

 

2.     人类染色体和黑猩猩染色体的比较

5.Comparison between human’s chromosomes and chimpanzee’s chromosomes

 

全世界人染色体的一致性，正是染色体恒定性的表现。最早人类祖先―“科学亚当”的染色体基本就是现代人的染色体。

 

The consistency of human chromosomes in the world reflects the constancy of chromosomes. The chromosomes of the earliest human ancestor, “Scientific Adam”, were basically those of the modern people.

 

同样，全世界黑猩猩染色体的一致性，也是染色体恒定性的表现。现代黑猩猩的染色体，同样就是最早黑猩猩祖先―“首位起始的黑猩猩”的染色体。

 

Meanwhile, the consistency of chimpanzee’s chromosomes in the world reflects the constancy of chromosomes. Chromosomes of modern chimpanzees are also those of the “initial chimpanzee”, i.e. the earliest chimpanzee’s ancestor. 

 

因此，我们和黑猩猩形态的差异，也是“科学亚当”和“首位起始的黑猩猩”形态的差异。

 

Therefore, the structural difference between chimpanzees and us is also that between “Scientific Adam” and “the first initial chimpanzee.”

 

人和黑猩猩染色体的差异表现在:染色体数目的差异（23对VS.24对）；染色体长度的差异；染色体着丝粒位置的差异；各染色体上基因数目和种类的差异；即使有类似功能的基因，它们在染色体的位置上也是不同的。因为染色体的恒定性，可以推论出，人和黑猩猩在染色体上的这些差异，自古以来，也一定是恒定的，因此，人和黑猩猩决不可能出于一个共同的祖先。

 

The chromosomal difference between human beings and chimpanzees lies in the chromosome number (23 pairs vs. 24 pairs), chromosome length; position of chromosomal centromere as well as gene number and type on all chromosomes. Even with similar functions, the genes have different positions on the chromosomes. From the constancy of chromosomes, we can infer that the chromosomal difference between human beings and chimpanzees must be constant in remote past. Therefore, it is impossible for human beings and chimpanzees to share one common ancestor.

 

 

 

图11-7 人类染色体和黑猩猩Y染色体比较

Figure 11-7  Comparison between Human’s Y Chromosome and Chimpanzee’s Y chromosome

 

在图11-7上，可以看到人和黑猩猩Y染色体的巨大差别。人Y染色体的长度大约是57MB ，有458个基因；黑猩猩是26MB，有278个基因。基因的种类也有很大差别，即使有类似功能的ZFY基因，在人，是位于Y染色体短臂 上端Yp11.3 处（短臂11带第3亚带，图上没有标出）。黑猩猩在长臂的底端（没有标出染色体的带号）。（详见本书附录）

 

Figure 11-7 shows the huge difference of the Y chromosomes between human beings and chimpanzees. The length of human Y chromosomes is about 57MB, containing 458 genes; the length of chimpanzee Y chromosomes is about 26MB, containing 278 genes. Types of genes are also very different. ZFY genes of similar functions, if any, are located on the upper of the short arm of Y chromosome, Yp 11.3 (i.e. Short Arm Band 11 sub-band 3, not marked in the picture), for human beings, and the bottom of long arm (without mark of chromosome band No.) for chimpanzees (See the Appendix of this Book)

 

从染色体恒定性上看，从古到今人进化了吗？黑猩猩进化了吗？我们能够和24对染色体的黑猩猩共祖吗？如果有这个共同祖先的“类人猿”，那么它是多少对染色体呢？它的染色体难道就不恒定吗？这些问题，是进化论无法面对的问题。

 

Have human beings evolved according to the constancy of chromosomes? Have chromosomes evolved? Can we share ancestor with chimpanzees of 24 pairs of chromosomes? How many pairs of chromosomes does the common ancestor, “Man Ape”, if existed once,  Were its chromosomes not constant? The Theory of Evolution cannot answer these questions.
