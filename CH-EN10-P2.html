六.染色体对数的差异与物种的各从其类

VI. Difference of chromosome pair numbers and each species according to its kind

 

在关于人类是否由进化产生的问题上，前面各节已经做了充分的论述。人和黑猩猩染色体对数的差别，是进化无法跨越的鸿沟。而且，也不可能是通过骤变来实现的。在其它的动物起源上，染色体对数同样是进化不可跨越的鸿沟。每一个染色体都是一个DNA编码的序列，染色体对数的差异，也就是DNA编码的差异，而且是巨大的差异。对染色体对数的分析，也是对DNA解码的分析，是“DNA编码与解码之物种起源学”重要的研究内容。

 

In the previous parts, we have discussed in great detail whether or not human beings have been generated by evolution. Difference of the chromosome pair numbers between human beings and chimpanzees  is an insurmountable gulf for the evolutionists. Moreover, it cannot be realized through saltation. About the origin of other animals, chromosome pair number is also an insurmountable gulf for the evolutionists. Every chromosome has a DNA coding sequence. The difference of chromosome pair number is also difference of DNA codes, even a large difference of DNA codes. Analysis on chromosome pair number is also on DNA decoding and together they constitute the important research contents of the discipline known as “the origin of species with DNA coding and decoding.”

The above table shows the difference of chromosome numbers in different animals. The Theory of Evolution cannot explain how evolution can fill up these “gulfs of chromosome numbers”. Since individual living beings of the same species have very consistent DNA sequences, every species comes from one and only the individual. Therefore, the theory of saltation cannot explain how the individual living being generated by saltation can replace a huge population. As we have already discussed about human origin in the previous parts, the differences in DNA codes and chromosome numbers have put an end to both the Theory of Evolution and the theory of saltation. The same conclusion is also applicable to other non human species.

 

图10-2是进化论下，各动物和人的进化树状图。它是为了描述一切生物都有着进化的联系，全都来自同一个根。但是，当给出了各种动物的染色体数目后，它们彼此之间已经被“鸿沟”所隔绝，不可能有着进化的联系。

 

Figure 10-2 is the evolution tree of all animals and human beings based on the Theory of Evolution which tries to show that all living creatures are connected in evolution and from the same root. However, after seeing the  chromosome numbers of all animals, they are separated from each other by the “gulf” which makes it impossible for any evolutionary connection.

 

虽然有少数动物的染色体数目相同，但是由于染色体和线粒体DNA的差别，仍然不可能有进化的联系。例如，狮子和老虎有着同样的染色体对数，它们虽然可以交配产生出下一代的狮虎（或虎狮），依然不可能产生出狮虎（或虎狮）物种。这就是DNA因素的物种隔离，如果没有这种隔离，世界上就会出现远比现在为多的、千千万万种、奇奇怪怪的动物。

 

Although chromosome numbers of a few animals are identical, their evolutionary connection is still impossible due to differences in chromosomal and mitochondrial DNAs. For example, lions and tigers with the same chromosome pair numbers can produce the next generation of liger (or tiglon) by mating, but they cannot produce a population of ligers (or tiglons). This is separation of species due to DNA factors. Without such separation, the world will have many bizarre animals far more than those existing  today.

 

结论是：各种生物都是源自各自的“一对源父母”，“源父母”相互之间，完全没有进化连接的关系。

Our conclusion is this: All living creatures come from their respective “one couple of source parents”, which completely have no evolutionary connection with each other.

七.欧洲鳗鱼和美洲鳗鱼有序的一生

VII. Ordered life of European Eel (Anguilla anguillas)  and  American Eel (Anguilla rostratas )

图10-3 鳗鱼的生命历程

Figure 10-3 Life of Eels

 

鳗鱼是一种很特殊的鱼类，尽管关于鳗鱼已经有超过6500种的出版物，它的生命史仍然是一个谜。在长达一千多年的历史中，人们根本不知道鳗鱼在什么地方诞生，只知道在河流海口的地方可以捕捞到鳗鱼苗，这些鳗鱼苗可以人工养殖，成为商品。多年的研究，才初步认识了它们生命的历程。

 

Eels are a very special kind of fish. Though over 6500 publications exist about eels, their life history is still a mystery. For over one thousand years, people remain ignorant about where eels are born. We only know that eels fry could be caught at estuaries and the eels fry could be developed into saleable products through artificial breeding.  Through many years of research, we have a preliminary understanding of the life of eels.

 

 以美洲鳗鱼和欧洲鳗鱼为例，这两种鳗鱼都诞生于大西洋的Sargasso Sea。马尾藻海只是被几条主要洋流围出的一个特定的区域，是一个“洋中之海”，也是世界上唯一一个没有海岸线的海。也许是因为在这个海面上，漂浮着大量马尾藻，使它成为了鳗鱼的产卵之地。一条条诞生的小鳗鱼，从这里开始了它们的生命历程。

 

Let us take the American eel and the European eel as the examples. Both of the eels are born in the Sargasso Sea in the Atlantic ocean. The Sargasso Sea is a “sea in the ocean” since it is a special area enclosed by several key ocean currents and also the only sea without a coast in the world. Maybe because of large amount of sargassum floating on the sea, it becomes an egg laying area for the eels. Many newly born eels started their lives from here.

 

小鳗鱼出生时只有3毫米长，随着海洋的洋流漂泊逐渐长大。可能是在长到20几毫米以后，才具有了掌握方向的能力。大约经过一年多的时间，美洲鳗鱼苗到达美洲沿岸的河口。而欧洲鳗鱼苗要经过两年左右，才能到达欧洲的河口，这是真正的万里长征。此时的小鳗鱼才长到70多毫米长，身体有些透明，因此被叫做玻璃鳗。玻璃鳗进入河流，逆流而上，开始了它们大半生的淡水生活。

 

Just 3mm long when a birth, young eels gradually grow by drifting with the ocean currents. After growing to over 20 mm, they acquire the ability to control their direction. For about one year later, American Eel reached the estuaries of the American coast. It takes European eel s about two years to reach the European estuaries. Indeed, this is a long march in its true sense. Meanwhile, the young eels grow just a little over 70 mm with quite transparent bodies, known as glass eels. The glass eels enter rivers and advance upstream to start the major part of the lives in fresh water.

 

 几年以后，它们长成近一米长的成熟鳗鱼，由于身体呈现黄色，被称作“黄鳗”。此时，一个不为人知的信息在它们心中苏醒，呼唤着它们开始了返回它们出生地的旅程。它们在河流中顺水而下，进入到大西洋。和幼年不同的是它们有了较强的游泳能力，加上海水“洋流”的作用,使它们可以在半年多的时间内,回到它们的出生之地—马尾藻海。在这样的旅途上，长期的体力消耗使它们变瘦、眼睛变大，皮肤由黄变成银色，因此被称为“银鳗”或“大眼睛鳗鱼”。

 

Years later, they will grow to nearly over one meter as mature eels with yellow bodies, known as “yellow eels”. Meanwhile, responding to yet an unknown signal which wakes them up and calls them to travel back to their original  birthplace. They drift downstream and enter the Atlantic ocean. Different from their childhood, they now have developed strong swimming capability. With the effect of “ocean current”, it takes them just a little over half a year to return to their birthplace, the Sargasso Sea. Due to a long physically consumption trip, they become thinner with larger eyes and silver bodies. Now, they are known as “silver eels” or “large-eye eels”.

 

 当它们到达马尾藻海后，雌、雄鳗鱼将完成它们生命中的最后使命，产卵和排精。新的生命诞生的时期，也是鳗鱼母亲和鳗鱼父亲生命的最后时期。它们的出生之地，成为了它们的葬身之地。

 

When they reach the Sargasso Sea, both female and male fishes will complete their last mission of life, i.e. to lay eggs and eject sperms. The birth of new lives is also the final period for the eel mother and eel father. Their original birthplace will become their final graveyard.

 

美洲鳗和欧洲鳗以 奇特的生活史吸引着众多从事鱼类研究的学者。它们在同一个海区诞生，大半生分别在各自大陆的淡水溪流中度过，成熟后便开始了长途迁徙，回到它们共同的诞生 地，几乎在一起产卵繁殖。半个世纪以来，人们一直在发出疑问：它们既然在同一水域繁殖，其交配是随机的吗？它们有着杂交吗？它们是否都有着各自生命旅程的 路线呢？如果是这样，它们是如何找到它们巡回的路线呢？这些问题长期困扰着众多的学者。

 

The American eel (Anguilla rostrata) and the European eel ( Anguilla Anguilla)  have attracted many scholars engaged in fish research due to their fascinating life cycle. Born in the same salt water ocean, they spend the majority of their lives in fresh water streams in perspective continents. Then, they start long migration when they become mature, return to their original birthplace, lay eggs and breed almost at the same time together. For over half a century, people have kept asking: Do their mate at random, since they breed in the same area? Is there any cross breeding with them? Do they have different life journey? If this is the case, how do they find their migration routes home to their original birthplace? Such questions have long perplexed many scholars.

 

 当分子人类学家通过DNA解码，来寻找人类来源和迁徙历程的时候，鱼类科学家们自然也想到了DNA解码这个具有权威的方法。美国University of Georgia的教授JOHN C. Avise等，付出了很大的努力，发表了报告“北大西洋鳗鱼线粒体的差异” [注释5]。

 

After the molecular anthropologists trace human origin and migration experience through DNA decoding, fish scientists naturally thought about using the most authoritative technique of DNA decoding. Professor John C. Avise from University of Georgia and others have published article“Mitochondrial DNA differentiation in North Atlantic eels” after a great deal of time and effort.（Reference5）

 

在这个报告中，分析了美洲鳗鱼和欧洲鳗鱼的线粒体DNA序列，发现各地区美洲鳗鱼线粒体DNA序列高度一

致。他们从美国最北部的缅因州到南部路易斯安娜州的7个地点，取得了109个美洲鳗鱼线粒体DNA的样本。从每个样本中，得到了55个DNA有效片段，包含了389个碱基对。

 

The article analyzes mitochondrial DNA sequences of American eel ( Anguilla rostrata) and European eel (Anguilla Anguilla)  and found that mitochondrial DNA codes of Anguilla rostrata are highly consistent in all regions. They collected 109 mitochondrial DNA samples of Anguilla rostrata. They obtained 55 effective DNA segments from each sample from the northern end of the United States, Maine, to the southern end, Louisiana, which contain 389 nucleotide base pairs.

 

 虽然这些鳗鱼样本分布在4000公里的海岸线上，它们的线粒体DNA都极为一致。在109个样本中，有73个样本的碱基字符，一字不差。其余30个样本，仅仅包含一个字符的差异。显示美洲鳗鱼非常可能是出于一个雌鳗鱼，即有一个“线粒体鳗鱼祖母”。

 

Although these eel samples were collected   along 4,000 km of coast, their mitochondrial DNAs were very consistent. Among the 109 samples, 73 have completely the same base nucleotides with the rest of the 30 samples with only 1 nucleotide difference. It shows that Anguilla rostrata is very likely to come from one female eel, i.e. one “mitochondrial eel grand  mother.”

 

 欧洲鳗鱼的线粒体DNA和美洲鳗鱼线粒体DNA编码十分一致，也有着差异（多态性 SNP），因此能够加以区分。尽管两种鳗鱼在一起产卵，但它们几乎没有发生混合。至于美洲鳗鱼和欧洲鳗鱼的关系，从线粒体DNA上来看，有44个片段碱基字符相同，占片段数目的60%。有可能它们是出于一个共同的线粒体的“起始源个体”，即有一位“起始的鳗鱼祖母”，但是还需要更多的染色体、线粒体DNA的编码来证实。

 

The mitochondrial DNAs of European eel and those of American eel are quite consistent with a little difference (SNP), with which the two can be distinguished from one another. Although laying eggs in the same place, almost no merging occur  between the two. As to the relationship between the American eel and European eel in terms of mitochondrial DNAs, 44 segments have identical base nucleotides accounting for 60% of the total segments. Perhaps they come from the same “original source ” with common mitochondria, i.e. one “initial eel grandmother”. To prove this hypothesis, we need more research on chromosome and mitochondrial DNA codes.

 

美洲鳗鱼和欧洲鳗鱼两个族群，它们各有各的巡游路线。它们一代代的遵循各自的几千哩的旅程，去同一个区域中交配产卵，却从不混合。它们的幼鱼，各回各家，从 不谜失，这里充满着无法理解的生命奥秘。显然，在同一个大西洋中，即使是欧洲鳗鱼去了美洲，或美洲鳗鱼到了欧洲，也决不会无法生存。从人类DNA编码中承载着“非物质的遗传信息”的事实，可以推论到鳗鱼身上，应该是鳗鱼DNA编码上的“非物质的遗传信息”，决定了各自的生命历程。因此，在美洲海岸没有发现欧洲鳗鱼，在欧洲也没有发现美洲鳗鱼。它们多少万年、多少代，都有着同样规律的一生，这些都是进化论无法解释的事实。

 

The two populations of the American and European eel have different migratory routes. They go to mate and lay eggs in the same area after travelling thousands of miles. However, there is no merging between them. The baby fish return to their own homes without getting lost. The eel’s life contains unexplainable mystery of life.  Obviously, in the same Atlantic ocean, even if the European eel reaches Americas or the American eel comes to Europe, they can still survive. The fact that human DNA codes carry “nonphysical genetic information” can be applied to the eels, i.e. the DNA codes of eels carry “nonphysical genetic information”, which determines the eel’s own individual life experience. Therefore, we have not found the European eel at the American coast nor American eel at the European coast. For tens of thousands of years and numerous generations, they have maintained the same life patterns which are facts unexplainable by the Theory of Evolution.

 

 DNA解码说出了生命的起源，但有关生命的来源以及类似鳗鱼这些习性包含的秘密，仍然是科学之谜。可以肯定地说，人们将永远无法从科学上得知生命的全部奥秘。

 

DNA decoding explains the origin of life, but the origin of life and the secret in the habits of eels and other animals are still a mystery of science. For sure, human beings will never unveil all the secrets of life with any discipline in science.

 

 八．小麦的祖先

VIII. Ancestor of wheat   

 

 现在，再来看一看植物的DNA编码。首先，让我们来关注和人类关系最为密切的小麦。

 

Now, let us take a look at the DNA codes of plants. First of all, let us pay attention to the wheat with the closest relationship with human beings.

 

农学家们一致地认为，目前的普通小麦，是原始的小麦Triticum monococcum和其它两种杂草杂交出来的品种。“Triticum monococcum”是14条染色体，当和Aegilop Spp（14条染色体）杂交后，生成28条染色体的Triticum dicoccoides 。Triticum dicoccoides再与Aegilops squarrosa（14条染色体）杂交，就生成了42条染色体的Triticum aestivum L。Triticum aestivum L实际包含了3个（A、B、D）基因组，也就是上面所说3个物种的3套染色体。每套染色体都有14条染色体，它们独立地存在于普通小麦细胞内，共同控制着普通小麦的生长。

Agronomists unanimously believe that the common wheats seen today are crossbreeding products of the original wheat, triticum monococcum, and two other weeds. Triticum monococcum with 14 chromosomes became Triticum dicoccoides with 28 chromosomes through cross breeding with Aegilop Spp (14 chromosomes). Through another crossbreeding in turn with Aegilops squarrosa (14 chromosomes), dicoccoides developed into Triticum aestivum L with 42 chromosomes, which actually contains 3 genomes (i.e. A, B and D) or 3 sets of chromosomes of the aforementioned 3 species. Every set of chromosomes has 14 chromosomes, which independently exist in cells of commonly known  wheats and jointly control their growth.

 

 无论是自然杂交或人工杂交育种，都不是进化的过程。因为不符合“经过无数的、连续的、轻微的变异形成的”这个进化论原则。

 

Either natural crossbreeding or artificial crossbreeding is not a process in evolution because it does not comply with the evolutionary principle of “being generated through numerous, continuous and tiny variations”.

 

早在上世纪70年代，有些农学家就提出A基因组起源于Triticum monococcum, 而另外一些学者却认为Triticum urartu才是A基因组的真正供体。野生的“Triticum monococcum”发现于新月沃土地区石器时代的遗址中，约公元前7000年左右在今土耳其东南部地区开始被人工栽种。乌拉尔图小麦最早也分布在那个区域。

 

As early as in 1970s, some agronomists suggested that Genome A comes from Triticum monococcum, while other scholars believe that Triticum urartu is the real donor of Genome A. The wild Triticum monococcum, discovered at

the Neolithic ruins of Fertile Crescent, started to be artificially planted in the southeastern part of the present day Turkey in about 7000B.C. At the very beginning, triticum urartu was also distributed in this area.

 

这个争论已经持续了几十年了，哪一个看法是正确的呢？谁是现代小麦的真正的祖先呢？显然，最好的、也是最简单的解决方法，仍然是依靠DNA解码。

 

This argument has lasted for dozens of years. Whose opinion is correct? Which one is the true ancestor of today’s wheat ? Obviously, the best and the simplest solution is to rely on DNA decoding.

当我们比较了这6百多个DNA编码序列以后，我们根据“DNA骰子”理论，可以毫不犹豫地说，这3个序列，它们都有一个原始的祖先：即唯一的一颗原始的“麦粒”。这颗原始的“麦粒”，Triticum monococcum的起始祖先。我们已经重复了很多次这个论断：随机地产生第二个如此一致的DNA编码，是不可能的。和动物不同的是，普通小麦是3个祖先结合的产物。这三个祖先DNA编码不是互相地转化，也不是相互的融合，而是各自独立地存在于同一个细胞核中，共同控制着小麦的生长。普通小麦已经有上万年的历史，在这几万年中，有多少粒小麦被繁殖出来，每一株小麦又有多少次的DNA复制过程。令人惊叹的是42条染色体在细胞中，都各自独立地复制，各染色体的编码序列，几乎没有什么改变。正是因为这样，我们才能看到基本相似的一粒粒的小麦。

 

After comparing the over 600 DNA codes, we can say without any hesitation that the above 3 sequences share the same original ancestor, i.e. the only original “wheat grain”. The original “wheat grain” is the initial ancestor of Triticum monococcum. We have repeated the conclusion many times: it is impossible to generate such identical DNA codes at random. Different from animals, ordinary wheat are products of combination of the 3 ancestors. Without mutual conversion or merger, DNA codes from the three ancestors independently exist in the same cell nucleus and jointly control growth of the wheat. Ordinary wheat have experienced nearly ten thousand years of history, during which numerous grains have been bred and DNA codes of every individual wheat have been replicated for millions of times. It is amazing that the 42 chromosomes are independently replicated in the cells with almost no change in the coding sequences of all chromosomes. As a result, we can see basically the same wheat grains.

这会是真的吗？其实，从一般道理上也可以看出，这会是真的。前面已经讲过，我们每一个人都是在60亿 编码的“调控”下，生长壮大的。我们有极其相似又有微小差别的编码，才有着各个人的样式。当你去查看小麦的麦粒时，你也会发现它们是多么的相似。如此的相 似，也必然是有着极其相似的编码。有着极其相似的编码，就意味着这不可能是在“进化理论”下，由随机形成的一致。那么，这极其相似的编码，就标志着它们全 是由最早的一组编码复制而来。

 

Is this really true? In fact judging from common sense, it may be true. The above parts we have already mentioned that every one of us develops and grows under the control of the “regulatory” 6 billion DNA codes. We each have our own physique with extremely identical and yet slightly different DNA codes. When examining the wheat grains, you will see that they are very identical. Such similarity must mean very identical DNA codes. The very similar DNA codes mean that the similarity cannot be generated at random as indicated in the Theory of Evolution. Then, the extremely similar DNA codes must mean that they are the product from one set of DNA codes in the very distant past.
